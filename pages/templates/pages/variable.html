Definition of manifolds and their interpretation.

Let us give the definition of manifold:

Assume $m\geq 0$ and $r = 0, 1, 2, \cdots, \infty$. "$\left( M, \left\{ U_{\alpha}, \varphi_{\alpha}, V_{\alpha} \right\}_{\alpha\in A} \right)$ is $m$-dimensional $C^r$ manifold" means following three conditions hold:
  • (1)
    $M$ is a Housdorff space. $U_\alpha\subset M$ and $V_\alpha\subset \mathbb{R}^m$ is a open set, and $\varphi_\alpha: U_\alpha\to V_\alpha$ is $C^r$-diffeomorphism for all $\alpha$,
  • (2)
    $\bigcup_{\alpha\in A} U_\alpha = M$,
  • (3)
    For all $\alpha, \beta\in A$,
    \[ \varphi_\alpha\circ\varphi_\beta^{-1}: \varphi_\alpha\left( U_\alpha\cap U_\beta \right) \to\varphi_\beta\left( U_\alpha\cap U_\beta \right) \]
    is $C^r$-diffeomorphism.
